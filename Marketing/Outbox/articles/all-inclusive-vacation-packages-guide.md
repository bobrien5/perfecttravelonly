## How to Get the Best Price on All-Inclusive Vacations

The difference between a good deal and a great deal often comes down to timing and strategy. Here's what actually works:

### Book During Shoulder Season
- **Best value months:** Late April, May, June, September, October, early November
- **Savings:** 25–40% off peak-season rates
- **Trade-off:** Slightly higher chance of rain (usually brief afternoon showers), fewer fellow guests (which is often a plus)

### Bundle Flights + Resort
Booking **all inclusive vacation deals** that include airfare almost always saves money versus booking separately. Charter packages from Apple Vacations, Funjet, and similar operators negotiate bulk rates you can't get on your own. Look for packages departing from your nearest gateway city.

### Watch for Flash Sales
Resorts run 48-hour and 72-hour flash sales — especially in January (New Year booking rush), late March (summer planning), and September (holiday travel). Sign up for our [VacationPro newsletter](/newsletter) to get these delivered to your inbox before they expire.

### Book Direct for Perks
OTAs often have the lowest base price, but booking direct with the resort can get you:
- Free room upgrades
- Resort credits ($50–$200)
- Late checkout
- Preferred room location

The total value of direct-booking perks frequently exceeds the $10–$30/night you might save on an OTA.

### Consider a Timeshare Preview
This is the insider move most people overlook. Many resorts — especially in Cancun, Punta Cana, and Jamaica — offer deeply discounted stays (sometimes 50–70% off) in exchange for attending a 90-minute resort presentation. You're under no obligation to buy anything.

## What's Actually Included in "All-Inclusive"?

Not all all-inclusive packages are created equal. Before you book, make sure you understand [what "all-inclusive" actually means](/blog/what-does-all-inclusive-mean) at your specific resort. Here's what's typically included and what often isn't:

### Usually Included
- **All meals** — Buffet and a la carte restaurants (number varies by resort)
- **Drinks** — House liquor, beer, wine, cocktails, coffee, juice, water
- **Non-motorized water sports** — Kayaks, paddleboards, snorkeling gear, sailboats
- **Entertainment** — Nightly shows, live music, pool activities
- **Pools and beach access** — Lounge chairs, towels, shade structures
- **Wi-Fi** — Most resorts now include this; a few still charge for premium/fast Wi-Fi
- **Tips and taxes** — Increasingly included, but verify

### Often NOT Included (Common Gotchas)
- **Premium or specialty restaurants** — Some resorts limit a la carte dining to a certain number of visits per week or charge a surcharge for their "signature" restaurant
- **Top-shelf liquor** — The included drinks may be house-brand only. Premium spirits, craft cocktails, and high-end wine often cost extra
- **Spa treatments** — Hydrotherapy circuits are sometimes included; massages, facials, and body treatments almost never are
- **Motorized water sports** — Jet skis, parasailing, banana boats, and scuba diving usually carry an additional fee
- **Off-site excursions** — Snorkeling tours, zip-lining, cenote visits, and island-hopping are virtually always extra
- **Resort fees** — Rare at true all-inclusive resorts, but some hybrid properties tack these on
- **Golf** — A few resorts include complimentary rounds (Hard Rock, Moon Palace), but most charge greens fees

**Bottom line:** Read the fine print on inclusions before you book. A resort at $200/night with everything included can be a better deal than one at $150/night that charges $30 for dinner and $8 per cocktail.
