#7

Booking flight and hotel separately.

Packaged vacation deals are almost always 15-30% cheaper than buying parts individually. The airlines don't want you knowing this.
Cost: $300-700

#6

Paying in USD abroad.

When the card machine asks "USD or local currency?" — ALWAYS pick local. Picking USD triggers a 3-7% markup called DCC. It's a trap.
Cost: $50-200/trip
VacationPro
#5

Skipping travel insurance.

One canceled flight, one hospital visit abroad, one lost bag — and you're out thousands. Trip insurance runs $40-80 and covers all of it.
Cost: up to $10,000+
VacationPro
#4

Booking flights on the wrong day.

Tuesday and Wednesday flights are consistently 10-20% cheaper than Monday or Friday. Travel mid-week, save hundreds per ticket.
Cost: $80-250/ticket
VacationPro
#3

Using a debit card abroad.

ATM fees + foreign exchange fees + bank fees stack fast. A travel credit card with zero FX fees saves 3-5% on every transaction.
Cost: $100-300/trip
VacationPro
#2

Not checking passport expiration.

Most countries require 6 months of validity beyond your return date. If you don't meet it, you'll be denied boarding — and there's no refund.
Cost: your entire trip

#1

Booking 2-3 weeks before the trip.

Worst possible window. Domestic flights are cheapest 1-3 months out. International? 2-8 months. Last-minute bookings cost 40%+ more.
Cost: $400-1,200
