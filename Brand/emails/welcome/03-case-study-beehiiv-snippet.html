A CASE STUDY
A deal we almost passed on.

Last week a Cancun package showed up at $487 — 4 nights, all-inclusive, airfare included. The headline was strong.

Then we ran our 3 checks.

Live pricing? Matched on the booking site. Good.

Historical range? Low end of the year. Good.

Inclusions? Here's where it almost failed. Airfare was only from 18 cities. If you lived in one of the other ~40 US metros, you were adding $200-300 for positioning flights. The "$487" became $687-$787.

We thought about skipping it. Instead we did what we always do: name the catch up front.

The writeup led with "Yes — but skip the flights." If you lived in a covered city, still a killer deal. If not, we told you to book flights separately and do the math.

THE RULE

Every deal writeup names the catch. If a package doesn't have a catch we'd flag, we either don't believe the pricing or we haven't looked hard enough. Honesty over hype.

— The VacationPro Team
